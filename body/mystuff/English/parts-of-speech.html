Parts of Speech

Introduction

  • A noun is a word used as a placeholder for a name place or a thing, here the thing includes anything that we can see, hear, touch, feel or smell and something we can think of, but cannot perceive by the senses.
  • An adjective is a word used to add something to the meaning of a noun.
  • A pronoun is a word used instead of a noun.
  • A verb is a word used to express an action or a state.
  • An adverb is a word used to add something to the meaning of the verb, adjective or another adverb.
  • A preposition is a word used with a noun or a pronoun to show how the person or thing denoted by the noun or pronoun stands in relation to something else.
  • A conjunction is a word used to join words or sentences.
  • An interjection is a word which expresses sudden feelings.
  • A determiner is a word which determines or limits the meanings of the nouns that follow.
